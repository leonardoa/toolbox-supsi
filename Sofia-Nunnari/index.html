Anti-Target

The "Anti-Target," a Creative Coding project that aims to provide users with a practical tool to avoid unwanted encounters (such as two exes agreeing not to see each other again). Using a mix of HTML, CSS, and JavaScript, this project offers alternative route suggestions integrated with maps from MapBox, a versatile platform for creating customized map experiences. When the person you'd like to avoid is nearby, the app will send you a personalized notification with directions for where to "escape" in real time.

WEB PLATFORM

This web platform shows the home page upon detection of your anti-target (such as your ex). Each time the page loads, the advice changes and is updated.

The red dot (as seen in the image before) represents your anti-target, while the white dot is your representation. By navigating the map and zoom out to get as far away from your anti-target as possible, clicking will display the route, duration time, and guided voice navigation.

CONCLUSIONS

This project was very challenging, even though the time available for programming was only one week (due to the class presentation of the project), it was creative work with its ups and downs during development.

After three days of programming I had to backtrack and reprogram the second part from scratch, it was hard but in the end I made it, and on the last day I was able to spend more time on the graphic design level as well.

Using MapBox for the first time was interesting, I wasted the first day of programming just to understand its documentation and use, to create small prototype maps, and to create the map style itself (which I used in the project).

Definitely, despite the complexity of MapBox but also its limitations, I am happy that I was able to develop my project with this tool.

The only flaw of MapBox is the lack of GPS navigation version (which it uses for cars) for the web version. I tried to recreate it manually myself but in the end (the third day announced before) I had to recreate that part completely because it was not supposed to be fake navigation but real. However, this helped me to manage and learn my "problem solving" and think quickly of an alternative that would be good and work.
